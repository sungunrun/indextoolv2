12. Taken alone, the perceptual repeatable representational competence to pick out
(refer to) a particular in an occurrent cause-dependent way—the schematic representational
competence that—is not discriminatory in the relevant sense. That is not a re peatable
type of representational response that is relevantly discriminatory. For every
particular in the occurrent causal chain leading to the occurrent perceptual state that
might in principle have been picked out, but is not, is a potential that. There is nothing in
the repeatable competence to pick out (refer to) particulars, in itself, that distinguishes the
particulars in the occurrent causal chain that are actually picked out from other particulars
that are not picked out, but might have been, given other supplementary competencies
than the ones that the perceiver actually exercises.148 (Premise)

13. So, in every perceptual state, there must be a further, more specific, repeatable representational
perceptual competence to respond representationally to repeatable types—
beyond the schematic that competence—that operates in perception, if a perceptual state
is to exercise a competence to pick out (refer to) particulars in the relevant discriminatory
way. The function of this further competence is to guide picking-out (referring)—discriminating
by its type some cause in the chain of occurrent causes from other occurrent
causes in the chain that are not perceived. (From 10., 11., and 12.)

14. So, in every perceptual state, both an occurrent exercise of the picking-out (referring)
competence—a referential application that occurrently exercises the that competence—and
an exercise of the supplementary representational perceptual competence to
discriminate types of particulars, must join in contributing to realizing a perceptual state’s
overall representational competence for being veridical, of fulfilling the veridicality conditions
that the perceptual state sets.149 (By 4., 6., 8., and 13.)
